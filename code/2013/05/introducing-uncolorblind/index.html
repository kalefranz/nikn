Introducing uncolorblind

POSTED May 13, 2013 by Kale Franz
TAGGED codecolor blindpythonqt
  • Share on Facebook
  • Share on Twitter
  • Share on Google+

I'm color blind. Or technically "color deficient." For example, most people can see the 2 in the color plate. I can't. (Yet I can study it closely and point out individual dots that look green-ish, orange-ish, or red-ish to me. Weird; I know.) So an utlra valuable tool for me in the past has been this WhatColor app. Not only does it provide the RGB and Hex values for the color on your screen directly below your mouse, it also tells you the name of the color. I'm working mostly on OS X now, and Apple's DigitalColor Meter just doesn't compete (or have the color name feature). tl;dr...I made my own; it's called uncolorblind, and it's all on github. I'll also package up distributable binaries at some point too.
