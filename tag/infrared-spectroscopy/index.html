Wavelength selection for quantum cascade lasers by cavity length

March 4, 2009   lasersQCLsinfrared spectroscopy
in Applied Physics Letters

A systematic shift in spectral emission wavenumbers in quantum cascade lasers (QCLs) is observed over a variation in cavity lengths from 0.5 to 3 mm resulting in a gain peak shift ranging from 2404 to 2286 cm−1. Thereby, a wavelength selection range of 118 cm−1 is provided, which is sufficiently broad for selecting the laser emission across the entire CO2 absorption band at 2326 cm−1 (4.3 µm). In contrast to current QCL wavelength selection techniques, modifying the cavity length is a straightforward postprocessing procedure.
